K-12 Tracks and Strands

The K-12 curriculum offers diverse paths for students to explore their interests and prepare for future careers. This page provides information and guidance on navigating the K-12 system and choosing the right career path.

Academic Track

This track is designed for students who plan to pursue higher education after senior high school. It offers four strands:

ABM (Accountancy, Business and Management)

This strand provides a foundation in business, finance, and management principles.

  • Accountancy
  • Business Administration
  • Marketing
  • Economics

HUMSS (Humanities and Social Sciences)

This strand focuses on developing critical thinking and communication skills in the context of humanities and social sciences.

  • Psychology
  • Sociology
  • Political Science
  • Literature

STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics)

This strand is designed for students with a strong aptitude for science and math who are interested in pursuing careers in these fields.

  • Engineering
  • Computer Science
  • Medicine
  • Research

GAS (General Academic Strand)

This strand offers flexibility for students who are still exploring their options and haven't decided on a specific career path.

  • Variety of electives
  • Broad foundation for college

TVL (Technical-Vocational-Livelihood) Track

This track equips students with job-ready skills for various industries. It offers different specializations, and students can earn National Certificates (NCs) that enhance their employability.

Home Economics

Focuses on skills related to housekeeping, tailoring, caregiving, food and beverage services, bread and pastry, tourism, and handicrafts.

Agri-Fishery Arts

Provides hands-on learning in agriculture and aquaculture, including food processing, rubber production, animal production, and landscape installation.

Information and Communications Technology (ICT)

Prepares students for careers in IT with skills in computer systems, programming, web development, and animation.

Industrial Arts

Equips students with skills in carpentry, automotive servicing, driving, electronics repair, electrical installation, welding, plumbing, and tile setting.

Other Tracks

Arts and Design Track

This track is for students interested in pursuing creative careers in:

  • Music
  • Theater
  • Visual Arts
  • Multimedia Arts
  • Dance

Possible college courses:

  • Fine Arts
  • Graphic Design
  • Architecture
  • Communication Arts

Sports Track

This track focuses on developing students' skills and knowledge in:

  • Safety and First Aid
  • Human Movement
  • Fundamentals of Coaching
  • Sports Officiating
  • Activity Management
  • Fitness, Sports and Recreation Leadership
  • Psychosocial Aspects of Sports
  • Fitness Testing
  • Exercise Programming
